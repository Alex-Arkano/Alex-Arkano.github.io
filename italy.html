Italy defeats Spain in a penalty shootout and reaches the Euro 2020 final

The first semifinal of the European Football Championship ended with the victory of Italy. The Italians managed to snatch a ticket to the final in a series of penalties.

At the beginning of the match both teams decided not to sit out in defense: in the 4th minute the Italians had a chance to excel - Niccolo Barella shot into the post. The referees, however, immediately recorded the offside.

After that, the Spanish team gradually took control of the ball, but the advantage in having the initiative in dangerous attacks did not grow: until the 20th minute, the teams struck only one shot on goal.

In the middle of the first half, the Italians had a good opportunity to excel. After the goalkeeper's unsuccessful exit, Roberto Mancini's charges only had to aim at the empty net, but they were late with the shot - the defenders of the Spanish national team managed to return and block Barrella's shot.

Since then and until the break, the situation on the field has not changed much. The Italians began to press the Spaniards a little more actively in their half of the field, but the dangerous blow came only in the last minute of the first half: Italian defender Emerson was in the opponents' penalty area and from an acute angle hit the goal, but hit the crossbar.

The teams went to the break with a score of 0: 0.

At the beginning of the second half of the match, Italy and Spain began to exchange blows more often, and in the 60th minute one of the attacks ended in a goal.

The Italians caught the Spaniards on a quick counterattack - Immobile cut a pass to Insigne, he could not pass the defense, but the ball bounced to Federico Chiesi, who shot a precise shot in the far corner. The goalkeeper of the Spanish national team did not have time to react.
