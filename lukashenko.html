Ukraine wants sanctions against Lukashenko's entourage. Who is on the list

The Cabinet of Ministers approved a proposal to the National Security Council on personal sanctions against a number of Belarusian citizens.

The list includes 52 people from Alexander Lukashenko's entourage.

The government explained that these sanctions are related to the autumn decision of the EU Council to impose sanctions against citizens involved in the falsification of the presidential election in Belarus.

The list also includes those involved in human rights violations, intimidation, repression, violent suppression of protests, torture and detention - in particular against Ukrainian citizens.

The government's proposal will be considered by the National Security Council within a month. If the National Security and Defense Council supports it, the decision must be signed by the president.

Relations between Kyiv and Minsk have remained strained since last year. Ukraine did not recognize the results of the election in Belarus, and Lukashenko - a legitimately elected president.

This year, Ukraine, like a number of other countries, also suspended flights to Belarus in response to the Belarusian authorities' interception of a Ryanair plane and the detention of opposition leader Roman Protasevich.
