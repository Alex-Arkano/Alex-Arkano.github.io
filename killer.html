Foreign hit squad killed Haiti's president, police say.

A group of 28 foreign mercenaries was involved in the assassination of Haitian President Jovenel Mois, police said.

Among them were 15 Colombians and two Americans of Haitian descent.

According to Police Chief Leon Charles, the search for eight more suspects continues.

Other members of the group were killed or arrested.

On Wednesday, they found a house where the suspects were hiding, it is located near the crime scene in the capital
Port-au-Prince.

The shootout with them lasted until night, and on Thursday the police captured most of the attackers.

Civilians assisted in the police search.

"We Haitians are shocked by what has happened, we do not accept it," one man told AFP. "We are ready to help because we need to know who is behind it, their names, their origins so that justice can do its part. case ".
